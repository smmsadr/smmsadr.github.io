## Context

Deriv's technology backbone is a large fleet of Linux services
running a real-time online trading platform. My work here was on the
platform side — the boring, essential job of keeping production
running while the trading and risk teams shipped features on top.

## Highlights

- Maintained the Linux environments and deployment tooling for
  production services, including package management, patching, and
  service configuration.
- Automated recurring operational tasks — deployments, health
  checks, log rotation, backups — to remove toil and increase
  reliability.
- Owned production support for critical services: monitoring,
  incident response, and post-mortems that fed back into the
  automation layer.

## Reflection

The classroom for systems thinking. You can read about distributed
systems, but nothing beats being the on-call human at 3am when they
misbehave. Every later modelling and optimization role has been
easier because of what production Linux taught me about how real
systems fail.
